## 3. Presentation, disclosure, and drafting flaws (non-numeric)

1. **Entity name inconsistency:** “Krishna **MobiIes**” (capital **I** instead of **l**) appears in multiple places — should be **Krishna Mobiles** throughout.  
2. **Corrupted characters:** “Auditors**ı** Report”, “Financia**l**” variants, “**l**NDEX”, “**partirs**” (related parties), spacing errors (“ac tivities”, “financi ng”, “equivale nts”).  
3. **Statement of financial position header:** column headings “From 01 Jan … to 31 Dec” belong to **income** statements; the balance sheet should use **“As at 31 December 2025”** and **“As at 31 December 2024”** (or comparative columns labelled clearly).  
4. **Auditor opinion vs accounting policies:** the auditor’s report refers to **IFRS for SMEs**; the accounting policies section refers to full **IFRS** promulgated by the IASB. The entity should **pick one framework** and align the opinion, basis of preparation, and any regulatory filing basis (UAE may require IFRS or IFRS for SMEs depending on criteria — confirm with engagement partner).  
5. **Note 3 — NBV caption:** “NBV as at 31st December, **2023**” should read **2025** (or the correct reporting date for that row).  
6. **Note 3 — Cost roll-forward:** opening is labelled **1 January 2024** while closing is **31 December 2025** with **no additions**; for clarity, opening for the PPE table should typically be **1 January 2025** (closing 2024 cost) unless this is intentionally a two-year table (then label both years explicitly).  
7. **Accounting policies §2.1:** “December 31, **.**” — incomplete date.  
8. **“Principal Activites”** — spelling: **Activities**.  
9. **Management report:** “AED 19079528.00  .” — use consistent thousands separators and spacing (**AED 19,079,528.00**).
